The whole or a division of goods, services or works to be procured.<br/><br/>Additional Information:<br/>Anything that can specify the procurement content (i.e. goods, services, work) is a Procurement Object. <br/>In a sense, such an "object" can constitute the "object of a contract".<br/>To test whether something is a Procedure Object check if it can have a Purpose and/or CPV classification (<i>The CPV establishes a single classification system for public procurement aimed at standardising the references used by contracting authorities and entities to describe the subject of procurement contracts.</i>).<br/><br/>Note:<br/>Procedure, seems to be an exception from this rule. Because it is a conflated term: it carries process properties and "purpose" propserties. <br/>

A forecast of the value of the procurement before competition.<br/><br/>Additional Information:<br/>Different cases of estimated values may refer to a lot, the global value of the procedure, or of a combinatorial value of a group of lots.<br/>The forecast is calculated by the buyer and covers all revenues  whether coming from the buyer or third parties.<br/>See for example recital (19), Article 5 of Directive 2014/24/EU and other articles from the rest of Directives about procurement.<br/><br/>In the case of framework agreements and dynamic purchasing systems this refers to the maximum estimated value.<br/><br/>This property corresponds to BT-27 in eForms (for Lot and Procedure) and can be used for BT-157 (for LotGroup).<br/><b><br/></b><b>WG Approval 05/12/2019</b><br/><br/><br/>
